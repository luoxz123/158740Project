## WebGIS and Spatial Decision Support

WebGIS platforms are useful for planning because they combine spatial data, interactive visualisation, and decision-support tools in a browser-based interface. Renewable energy planning benefits from WebGIS because users can compare suitability factors such as resources, grid access, terrain, and environmental constraints.

## Renewable Energy Suitability

Wind and solar siting commonly uses multi-criteria decision analysis. Important factors include wind speed, solar irradiance, slope, distance to transmission lines, distance to roads, land-use constraints, and protected areas.

## Geographic Information Retrieval

GIR links text documents to geographic locations. Place names in documents can be extracted using named entity recognition and then geocoded into spatial point layers. This project applies GIR to renewable energy articles so that news-mentioned locations can be compared with suitability zones.
